WCR Lexicon

The upcoming marriage of Grandula, High Elechon of the Belarans, and Sordani, Primaria of Tedorfell, will set aside centuries of strife to unite their kingdoms, establishing a new era of peace and allyship from Witchling Falls to the distant borders of the Cloudrift. In honor of this momentous occasion, the royal chronographer Sylphis Hedera has commissioned a new encyclopedia to record the joint history of their two kingdoms. They have assembled scholars and leximages from both kingdoms to put aside their differences and collaborate on a definitive historical account, starting from the mythical events of the Sundering Wars to the modern era.

&Round 7

Everyone grab your seats, and stop giving me that stink eye. I know we're on a schedule here, but it's not my fault if I'm called away on short notice for court-mandated community service. (They had inspecting the new prisons in the capitol for potential security weaknesses--at least they're right that I have the expertise to consult on that.) Fortunately, I just received word that the royal wedding is getting pushed back a few more weeks. Well, fortunate for us trying to finish this lexicon on time. Unfortunate for those poor government scribes trying to rewrite all the tariff agreements and smooth out the political kinks. I don't envy them... there are days when this whole unification business seems to be teetering on a saber's edge.

But to business! We've been adjourned for two weeks but I'm pleased to see that you haven't been slacking. It looks like our articles this week skewed heavily geographical and heavily southern. Miss Raptureridge brings us an articulate account of the Yelda Arch as a keystone trade route. Lady Cantabella-Everene speaks with appropriate admiration of Graefel, Tedorfell's most famous port city, and Ratalan calls upon his years of living there to tell us about the nation's capitol, Kol'adarash. Ser Caen meanwhile looks to the southern plains of Belar to tell us about Re'alro, or "The Switching City", though hm... Caen, I thought I warned you not to get too personal with the recent history. (And Griz, you may want to review this before it goes to print.) And speaking of recent history, Dr. Grenada has elected to write about a fellow of our ilk, the historian and poet Nogriff the Astute, while Barti has delivered a most colorful interpretation of the legacy of Festis and Questis.

One final item... I have taken the liberty of adding my own contribution to the codex this week. Yes, yes, I know we'd all prefer to ignore the embarrassing elephant in the room, but somebody needed to take the fall and write about The Umbridgement. It's a part of our history after all! An embarrassing, messy, regretful part, sure. Did I hate every minute of writing it? Sure! Definitely worth it to watch you all squirm now, though.

Gorthan Frim
